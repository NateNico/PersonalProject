The Systematic ongoing
Genocide of Amhara people

The Amhara people have been subjected to escalating violence that many describe as systematic genocide. Ethnic-based attacks have resulted in mass casualties, forced displacements, and widespread human rights violations. Villages have been destroyed, and countless lives have been lost in targeted assaults. This article explores the historical context of these conflicts, provides detailed accounts of recent atrocities, and emphasizes the urgent need for national and international intervention to halt the ongoing violence against the Amhara community.

The Systematic Loss
of Amhara Land & Wealth

Over the past decades, the Amhara people have experienced a systematic erosion of their ancestral lands and economic resources. Policies favoring other regions and ethnic groups have led to the redrawing of boundaries and the confiscation of fertile territories traditionally inhabited by Amharas. This loss of land has been accompanied by the destruction of livelihoods, cultural heritage sites, and economic disenfranchisement. Through an in-depth analysis of land policies, eyewitness accounts, and economic data, this article uncovers the strategies used to marginalize the Amhara population and the profound impact on their community.

Movements to Restore
Amhara Sovereignty

In response to the ongoing challenges faced by the Amhara people, various movements have emerged both within Ethiopia and among the global diaspora. These movements aim to advocate for the rights of the Amhara community, restore their sovereignty, and seek justice for the atrocities committed against them. Grassroots organizations, political groups, and international advocacy campaigns are working tirelessly to raise awareness, influence policy changes, and mobilize support. This article highlights these efforts, profiles key activists, and discusses the strategies being employed to bring about meaningful change for the Amhara people.
